Earth Guardians

Address: 123 Main Street, Boulder, Colorado, USA

Earth Guardians mobilizes youth and communities to combat climate change and protect biodiversity through education, advocacy, and sustainable practices.

Volunteering Opportunities

Clean-Up Crew Day

Earth Guardians invites volunteers to join our Clean-Up Crew Day, where we'll work together to remove litter and debris from local parks, promoting environmental stewardship and community involvement.

  • Type: Environmental
  • Age: Teen (12-17), Adult (18+)
  • Date and Time: Saturday, April 20th, 10:00 AM - 1:00 PM
  • Address: Eben G. Fine Park, 101 Arapahoe Ave, Boulder, CO 80302
Roles:
  • Clean-Up Crew Volunteers

    Assist in cleaning up litter and debris from designated areas.

    Volunteers will gather at the specified location and work together to collect and properly dispose of litter and debris, promoting a cleaner environment and healthier ecosystems.

Tree Planting Initiative

Earth Guardians is organizing a Tree Planting Initiative to combat climate change and improve air quality in urban areas. Volunteers will plant native tree species in designated locations, contributing to reforestation efforts and urban green spaces.

  • Type: Environmental
  • Age: Adult (18+), Family Friendly
  • Date and Time: Sunday, May 5th, 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  • Address:123 Main Street, Boulder, Colorado, USA
Roles:
  • Tree Planting Volunteers

    Assist in planting trees and maintaining planting sites.

    Volunteers will receive training on proper tree planting techniques and will work together to plant trees in designated areas. They will also assist in site preparation and maintenance to ensure the long-term health and survival of the newly planted trees.

Paws for Compassion

Address: 456 Oak Avenue, London, United Kingdom

Paws for Compassion rescues and rehabilitates abused, abandoned, and neglected animals, providing them with love, care, and forever homes while promoting responsible pet ownership.

Volunteering Opportunities

Adoption Day at the Shelter

Paws for Compassion is hosting an Adoption Day at our shelter, where volunteers will assist with caring for animals, interacting with potential adopters, and facilitating adoptions to find loving forever homes for our shelter animals.

  • Type: Animals
  • Age: Teen (12-17), Adult (18+), Family Friendly
  • Date and Time: Saturday, June 15th, 11:00 AM - 3:00 PM
  • Address: 456 Oak Avenue, London, United Kingdom
Roles:
  • Adoption Day Volunteers

    Assist with caring for animals and facilitating adoptions.

    Volunteers will help set up and maintain the adoption event area, care for shelter animals by providing food, water, and socialization, and assist potential adopters with the adoption process, including completing paperwork and answering questions about available animals.

Fundraising Pet Walkathon

Paws for Compassion is organizing a Fundraising Pet Walkathon to raise funds for animal welfare initiatives. Participants can enjoy a leisurely walk with their pets while supporting a great cause. The event will also feature pet-friendly activities, contests, and vendor booths.

  • Type: Animals
  • Age: Adult (18+), Family Friendly
  • Date and Time: Sunday, September 8th, 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  • Address: The Albert Memorial in Kensington Gardens, London W2 2UH, United Kingdom
Roles:
  • Walkathon Volunteers

    Assist with event setup, registration, and coordination during the walkathon.

    Volunteers will help set up registration and check-in stations, distribute event materials and route maps, assist participants with any inquiries, and ensure the smooth flow of the walkathon route. They may also help with crowd control, water stations, and cleanup after the event.

Hope for Humanity

Address: 789 Hope Street, Nairobi, Kenya

Hope for Humanity empowers marginalized communities in Kenya through education, healthcare, and sustainable development projects, fostering long-term prosperity and social justice.

Volunteering Opportunities

Community Health Fair

Hope for Humanity is hosting a Community Health Fair to provide free health screenings, educational workshops, and access to community resources. Participants can receive screenings for blood pressure, diabetes, and other common health concerns, attend workshops on nutrition and wellness, and connect with local organizations offering support services.

  • Type: Healthcare
  • Age: Adult (18+), Family Friendly
  • Date and Time: Saturday, October 15th, 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM
  • Address: 789 Hope Street, Nairobi, Kenya
Roles:
  • Event Volunteers

    Assist with event setup, registration, workshop coordination, and cleanup.

    Volunteers will help set up tents, tables, and chairs for the event, assist with participant registration, guide attendees to different areas of the fair, and help with workshop coordination. They may also assist with distributing informational materials, managing lines, and cleaning up the venue after the event.

Hope Kits Distribution Drive

Hope for Humanity is organizing a Hope Kits Distribution Drive to assemble and distribute essential kits to families in need. These kits include food items, hygiene products, school supplies, and other necessities to support families facing economic hardship. Volunteers will help assemble kits, organize distribution logistics, and deliver kits to designated locations.

  • Type: Social
  • Age: Adult (18+), Family Friendly
  • Date and Time: Saturday, November 20th, 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM
  • Address: 789 Hope Street, Nairobi, Kenya
Roles:
  • Kit Assembly Volunteers

    Assist with assembling Hope Kits by packing donated items into bags or boxes.

    Volunteers will work in teams to assemble Hope Kits by packing donated food items, hygiene products, and other essentials into bags or boxes. They will follow assembly instructions and ensure that each kit contains the necessary items. Volunteers may also help organize inventory, label packages, and prepare kits for distribution.

  • Distribution Volunteers

    Help with organizing and distributing Hope Kits to families in designated areas.

    Volunteers will assist with organizing and distributing Hope Kits to families in designated areas. They may help load kits onto vehicles, navigate delivery routes, and distribute kits at distribution sites. Volunteers will ensure accurate record-keeping and coordinate with local community leaders to identify families in need of assistance.
